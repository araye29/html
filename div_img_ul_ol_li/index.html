Nelson Mandela
Biography:
Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela (1918–2013) was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary and political leader who became the first black President of South Africa in 1994.
Major Achievements:
  1. Led the struggle against apartheid in South Africa
  2. Spent 27 years in prison for his activism
  3. Won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993
  4. Became South Africa’s first democratic president in 1994
  5. Promoted peace and reconciliation worldwide
Personal Interests:
  • Human rights advocacy
  • Education and youth empowerment
  • Sports and teamwork
  • Community service
Legacy:
Nelson Mandela is remembered as a symbol of peace, justice, and equality across the world.
